# Post-Operative Hypertension 
---

## I. Definitions  

- **Hypertensive urgency:** SBP ≥180 mmHg or DBP ≥120 mmHg **without** acute end-organ damage.  

- **Hypertensive emergency:** SBP ≥180 mmHg or DBP ≥120 mmHg **with** evidence of acute end-organ damage.  

---

## II. Evaluation  

### Step 1 – Confirm  
- Repeat BP; ensure correct cuff size, patient positioning, and rest.  
- If arterial line present, confirm accuracy.  

### Step 2 – Assess for End-Organ Damage  
- **Neurologic:** agitation, delirium, stupor, seizures, visual changes, focal deficits.  
- **Cardiac:** chest pain, ACS, elevated troponin, ischemic ECG.  
- **Aortic:** tearing chest/back pain (consider dissection).  
- **Pulmonary:** dyspnea, pulmonary edema, hypoxia.  
- **Renal:** oliguria, elevated creatinine, abnormal BMP/LFTs/BNP.  

### Step 3 – Focused Testing  
- BMP, LFTs, CBC.  
- Troponin, BNP.  
- ECG, chest X-ray.  
- CT brain if focal neuro deficits.  
- CTA chest/abdomen if dissection suspected.  

---

## III. Management Principles  

### General Targets  
- **Hypertensive urgency:** lower BP by **10–20% over first 2–4 hours**, goal near **160/110**, then normal over 24–48h.  
- **Hypertensive emergency:** lower **MAP by ≤25% in first hour**, then to **~160/100** over 2–6h, then gradual normalization in 24–48h.  

### Exceptions  
- **Acute Ischemic Stroke**  
  - If reperfusion candidate: treat only if >185/110.  
  - If not reperfusion candidate: treat only if >220/120.  
- **Aortic Dissection**  
  - Rapidly lower to **SBP 100–120** within minutes.  
  - Control **HR 60–80** first with IV β-blocker, then add vasodilator.  

---

## IV. Stepwise Management  

### Step 1 – Fix Reversible Triggers  
- Pain, anxiety, urinary retention, hypoxia, hypercarbia, hypothermia.  
- Restart **home antihypertensives** at appropriate formulation/dose/timing.  
- If missed dialysis → nephrology for urgent dialysis.  

### Step 2 – Oral Therapy (for stable patients, no organ injury)  
- **Captopril PO**: 12.5–25 mg q8h (captopril:lisinopril = 5:1).  
- **Hydralazine PO**: 10–20 mg q6h.  
- **Isosorbide dinitrate PO**: 5–20 mg TID.  
- **Nifedipine XL PO**: 30 mg daily, titrate (max 90 mg BID).  
  - *Do NOT use sublingual/short-acting nifedipine.*  
- **Clonidine PO**: 0.1–0.2 mg, then 0.05–0.1 mg q1–2h (max 0.6–0.7 mg); caution sedation/rebound.  

### Step 3 – IV Therapy (for emergencies or severe symptoms)  
- **Labetalol IV**: 10–40 mg q20–30 min; or infusion 1–2 mg/min.  
- **Hydralazine IV**: 10–20 mg q20–30 min; avoid in dissection, unpredictable response.  
- **Nicardipine infusion**: start 5 mg/h; titrate q5–15 min to max 15 mg/h.  
- **Clevidipine infusion**: 1–2 mg/h; titrate rapidly; max 16–32 mg/h (watch lipid load).  
- **Nitroglycerin infusion**: 5–10 mcg/min, titrate (best for ACS, pulmonary edema).  
- **Nitropaste 1”**: apply q6h; titratable (wipe off for effect); bridge until oral regimen in place.  
- **Enalaprilat IV**: 1.25 mg q6h (up to 5 mg); avoid in AKI/hyperkalemia.  

### Step 4 – Transition  
- Once controlled, transition to oral long-acting regimen (amlodipine, ACEi/ARB, β-blocker, thiazide).  
- Adjust doses upward to prevent rebound hypertension.  

---

## V. Special Situations  

| Scenario | First-line | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Aortic dissection** | Esmolol → add nicardipine/clevidipine | SBP 100–120, HR 60–80 |
| **Acute ischemic stroke** | See stroke-specific thresholds | Only treat >185/110 (thrombolysis) or >220/120 |
| **ACS/ischemia** | Nitroglycerin infusion ± β-blocker | Avoid excessive DBP lowering |
| **Pulmonary edema** | Nitroglycerin infusion, diuretics, NIPPV | Rapid BP titration often needed |
| **Pregnancy (pre-/eclampsia)** | Labetalol, hydralazine, nicardipine; MgSO₄ if eclampsia | Avoid ACEi/ARB, nitroprusside |
| **Missed dialysis** | Urgent dialysis | Treat volume overload directly |

## VI. Quick Reference Medications  

### IV Infusions  
| Drug | Start | Max | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nicardipine | 5 mg/h, ↑2.5 mg/h q5–15 min | 15 mg/h | Reflex tachy, volume overload |
| Clevidipine | 1–2 mg/h; double q90s initially | 16–32 mg/h | Contra: egg/soy allergy, severe AS |
| Esmolol | 500–1000 mcg/kg load → 50–200 mcg/kg/min | — | Good for HR control, dissection |
| Nitroglycerin | 5–10 mcg/min infusion | Institutional max | For ACS/pulmonary edema |

### IV Bolus  
| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Labetalol | 10–40 mg IV q20–30 min (≤300 mg) | Good if tachycardic |
| Hydralazine | 10–20 mg IV q20–30 min | Avoid in dissection, reflex tachy |
| Enalaprilat | 1.25 mg IV q6h (up to 5 mg) | Avoid in AKI, hyperkalemia |

### Oral (for urgency/stable pts)  
| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Captopril | 12.5–25 mg q8h | Avoid in AKI/hyperkalemia |
| Hydralazine | 10–20 mg q6h | Unpredictable |
| Isosorbide dinitrate | 5–20 mg TID | Use in combination |
| Nifedipine XL | 30 mg daily (max 90 BID) | Not sublingual |
| Clonidine | 0.1–0.2 mg, then 0.05–0.1 mg q1–2h (max 0.6–0.7 mg) | Sedation, rebound risk |

## VII. Red Flags – Call Senior/ICU  
- SBP ≥180 or DBP ≥120 **with end-organ damage**.  
- Concern for **aortic dissection** or **acute stroke**.  
- Refractory BP despite multiple IV meds.  
- Pregnant/postpartum patient with severe hypertension.  

---

## VIII. Intern Pearls  
- Always fix **pain, anxiety, bladder, hypoxia, missed meds** before escalating IV therapy.  
- **Avoid precipitous drops**: lower MAP ≤25% initially (except dissection).  
- For **urgency**, oral meds suffice — no need for IV drips.  
- For **emergency**, use **IV infusions** for smooth titration.  
- Restart and **optimize home regimen** early to prevent rebound.  
- **Nitropaste** is a helpful bridge in the early post-op setting.

## Key Takeaways  
- **Urgency = severe BP, no damage → oral therapy, gradual lowering.**  
- **Emergency = severe BP + end-organ damage → ICU, IV titration.**  
- **Exceptions:** aortic dissection (rapid), stroke (per protocol).  
- **Reversible causes and missed meds/dialysis are common post-op triggers.**  
- Transition to oral, long-acting control early.
